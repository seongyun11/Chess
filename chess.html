Chess Pieces



      Pawn

The most common piece on the board. It can move
one square forward and ONLY capture one square sideways.
It may move two squares only when it hasn't moved from its
starting position. When a pawn reaches the end of a board, it
may promote to any piece (queens, bishops, rooks, etc) except
a king.



Knight

The knight moves in a peculiar way that looks like a capital L
and can jump over pieces. It can capture any piece that happens
to be in its way.








Bishop

The bishop can only move in any square diagonal to it. It can
capture the same way as it moves.










Rook

The rook can move anywhere in a straight line. It can castle with
the king when the king has not moved. It can capture the same way as it moves.










Queen

The queen can move anywhere that is connected in a
diagonal, straight line, or one square in any direction.
It is the most powerful piece, but not the most important.









King

The king is the most important piece. If it gets trapped while targeted and
gets checkmated, the game ends. The king can only move one square in any
direction. It can take the same way as it moves. It can castle if it doesn't move.
